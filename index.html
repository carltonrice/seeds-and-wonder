Morning — Caleb 1:1 Time

💧 Morning Water Play

15-20 min · Sensory + Fine Motor After breakfast, before it gets hot
›

What You'll Need

  • Shallow bin or large baking dish with 1-2 inches of water
  • Measuring cups, spoons, a small funnel if you have one
  • A few small toys (rubber duck, plastic animals, balls)
  • Towel underneath for spills

How To Do It

Set up outside on the patio or porch while it's still cool (before 10am). Let Caleb stand or sit at his water table or a low bin. Drop in a few toys and cups. That's it — let him lead.

At 18 months, the magic is in the pouring, scooping, and splashing. He's learning cause and effect every time water moves. Name what he does: "You're POURING! Look at the water GO!"

🐻 Caleb (1y6m) — What to watch for Does he transfer water between cups? That's early problem-solving. Does he dunk toys and pull them out? That's object permanence practice. Does he splash with both hands? Bilateral coordination. Everything he does here is developmental gold. Just narrate and enjoy.
🔄 If You Skip This
  • Too hot already? Do it inside in the kitchen with a towel mat. Smaller bin, same fun.
  • Not feeling water today? Swap for a dry sensory bin: dried pasta + measuring cups + spoons (same fine motor, no mess cleanup).

🎵 Dance Party + Instruments

10-15 min · Music + Gross Motor Mid-morning (inside, AC)
›

What You'll Need

  • Phone or speaker for music
  • Wooden spoon + pot lid (instant drum kit)
  • Shaker: sealed container with dried pasta or rice inside

How To Do It

It's going to be 89°F — stay inside for this one. Put on some toddler-friendly music and let Caleb bang, shake, and dance. Follow his lead. If he's banging the pot, bang along. If he's bouncing, bounce with him.

This is gross motor + rhythm + joy. No agenda. Just move together.

Freeze Dance — The Kiboomers
Caleb loves the stop-and-go — great for impulse control too
If You're Happy and You Know It — Super Simple Songs
Classic clap-along — he'll try to mimic the movements
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) — What to watch for Can he bang in rhythm (even loosely)? That's early musical timing. Does he try to clap or stomp when the song says to? That's following directions. Does he bring you a "drum"? That's sharing and social engagement. All wins.
🔄 If You Skip This
  • Not in the mood? Just put music on during free play. Background music still counts.
  • Save for Logan: Redo this when Logan gets home — he'll love leading the dance party for Caleb.

🎨 Sidewalk Chalk Art

15-20 min · Art + Fine Motor + Together Late afternoon (shade!)
›

What You'll Need

  • Sidewalk chalk (thick pieces for Caleb's grip)
  • Driveway or sidewalk in the shade
  • Water cups + paintbrushes (optional: "paint" with water to erase and redo)

How To Do It

Wait for late afternoon shade (after 4pm if possible — it's going to be hot). Both boys together. Logan draws, Caleb scribbles. That's the whole plan.

Bonus: Draw a simple road and let them "drive" toy cars on it. Or trace their hands. Or draw animals from yesterday's creek adventure and have Logan tell Caleb about them.

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Let him draw whatever he wants. Ask "What's that?" and write the word next to it (early literacy connection). He can trace letters or draw shapes. Challenge: "Can you draw a fish like the ones in the creek yesterday?" Connects today to yesterday's experience.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) He'll grip the chalk in a fist and make marks. That IS writing at 18 months. He might eat the chalk — redirect gently. Let him scribble over Logan's drawings (Logan may not love this — coach sharing). Water "painting" with a brush is a great alternative if chalk is frustrating.
🔄 If You Skip This
  • Still too hot? Do coloring or painting inside at the table instead.
  • Logan needs solo time? Totally fine. Let him have free play. Caleb can do chalk solo or skip to evening wind-down.

Evening Wind-Down

🌙 Cozy Friday Story Time

15 min · Language + Bonding Before bed
›

What You'll Need

  • 2-3 favorite books (let Logan pick one, pick one for Caleb)
  • Blanket fort, couch pile, or floor pillows
  • Stuffed animals welcome

How To Do It

End the week cozy. Build a little nest on the couch or floor. Both boys in laps or snuggled up. Read slow. Let Logan "read" to Caleb if he wants — he loves being the teacher.

After books: "What was your favorite part of today?" For Logan, this builds reflection. For Caleb, just naming things he saw ("WATER! CHALK!") is enough.

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Ask him about school today. What did he do? Who did he play with? If he doesn't want to talk about it, that's OK — he might open up during story time. Let him narrate the pictures in the book.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) He'll be tired. Short and sweet. Let him hold a book and turn pages even after you're done reading. If he falls asleep during story time, that's the best possible outcome.
🔄 If You Skip This
  • Everyone's wiped? Bath and bed. The week was full. You crushed it.
  • Logan wants screen time? One short show together is fine for Friday. Bluey counts as quality content.
