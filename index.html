Morning

🦁 Quick Animal Sort

10 min · Science + Fine Motor Before Bible study
›

What You'll Need

  • Animal toys from the toy bin (the ones they already have)
  • 3 bowls or plates for sorting groups

How To Do It

Quick and easy before Bible study. Dump the animal bin on the floor. "Can you sort them? Big animals here, little animals there!" That's it. Let them play with the sorting for 10 minutes, then clean up is part of the activity: "Can you put all the animals back in the bin before we go?"

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Sort by type (farm vs. jungle vs. ocean) or by size. Count each group. "Which group has the most?" Let him name every animal as it goes in.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) He picks up animals and drops them in bowls. Name each one: "LION! Rawr!" The dumping and dropping IS the activity for him. Celebrate every drop.
🔄 If You Skip This
  • Totally fine. Bible study is the main morning event. This is just a warm-up if there's time.
  • Save for evening: Animal toys can be the bedtime pretend play instead.

Afternoon

🌊 Pfluger Park Creek Play

All afternoon · Sensory + Gross Motor + Social After nap
›

What To Bring

  • Water shoes for both boys
  • Change of clothes + towel
  • Sunscreen (upper 80s, clearing skies)
  • Snacks and water bottles
  • Optional: small bucket or cup for scooping

What Creek Play Builds

This isn't just splashing. Creek play is a developmental goldmine:

  • Sensory processing: Cold water on feet, smooth rocks, muddy textures, the sound of flowing water
  • Balance and coordination: Walking on uneven creek beds, stepping on rocks, crouching to pick things up
  • Social skills: Playing alongside friends, sharing space, taking turns with good spots
  • Science exploration: Water flow, what sinks vs. floats, where bugs live, how rocks feel different
  • Language: Naming what they see, describing textures ("slimy!", "cold!", "bumpy!")

Nature Scavenger Hunt

While you're there, see how many of these you can spot together:

  • 🪨 A smooth rock
  • 🐛 A bug (any kind)
  • 🍂 A leaf floating on the water
  • 🐦 A bird
  • 🌿 Something green growing near the water
  • 🦎 A lizard or frog (bonus!)
  • 💧 A splash bigger than your hand
  • 🐾 An animal print in the mud (bonus!)
🦖 Logan (3y3m) He'll want to go deeper. Let him explore but stay close. He can do the scavenger hunt. "Can you find a smooth rock? How about a bug?" Count his finds. Let him throw rocks in the water (away from other kids). Ask: "Which rock makes the biggest splash?"
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Shallow edge only. He'll sit and splash. Let him pick up rocks and drop them in the water. The cold water on his feet is a huge sensory experience. Stay within arm's reach. Name everything: "WATER! COLD! SPLASH!" He'll be fascinated just watching the water flow.

Evening Wind-Down

📖 Animal Story + Pretend Play

15 min · Language + Imagination Before bed
›

What You'll Need

  • Dear Zoo by Rod Campbell, or any animal book with flaps
  • A few animal toys from this morning's sort
  • Cozy spot: couch, blanket fort, or floor pillows

How To Do It

After bath time, settle in with a book. Read together, nice and slow. If you have matching animal toys, hold them up as they appear in the story. This connects the morning sort to the evening wind-down.

After the book, let them do 5 minutes of quiet pretend play with the animal toys. "Where are your animals going to sleep tonight?" Make little beds out of washcloths. Tuck the animals in. Then tuck the boys in.

🦖 Logan (3y3m) He can retell what happened at the creek today. "What animals did you see at the park?" Let him "read" the book to Caleb or to his stuffed animals. He loves being the big brother teacher.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Lap time. Let him turn pages and point. He'll hold one animal toy the whole time. Name everything softly. This is calm-down time. Short is fine. If he's tired from the park, even 5 minutes counts.

🔄 Alternatives
  • Too tired for a book? Just the animal pretend play. 5 minutes of quiet play with toys is enough.
  • Don't have Dear Zoo? Brown Bear Brown Bear, Good Night Gorilla, or any animal book works.
  • Both boys wiped? Skip this entirely. The creek was the whole day. Bath and bed is fine.
