🔍 Seed Detective Agency

15–20 min · Science + Math
›

What You'll Need

  • Dried beans or lentils from the pantry
  • An apple (or any fruit with visible seeds)
  • Paper plate or muffin tin for sorting
  • Damp paper towel + ziplock bag

How To Do It

Cut open the apple and show the kids the seeds hiding inside. "Look — the apple was keeping a secret!" Let them touch and explore.

Spread out beans, lentils, and apple seeds. Sort by size or color. Count together as you go. Tuck a few beans into the damp paper towel, slide into ziplock bag, tape to a sunny window — you've got a seed observation station!

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Let him lead the sorting — "Can you put all the big ones here?" Count each group. Ask: "Which seed will sprout first?" Let him tape the bag to the window himself.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Let him feel the seeds (supervise — no mouthing). Muffin tin for dropping beans into = great pincer grasp. Let him pat the wet paper towel. Name everything: "Seed! Bean! Wet!"
How Seeds Grow — SciShow Kids

🔄 Alternatives & If You Don't Have It
  • No apple? Use a bell pepper, tomato, or watermelon — any seeded fruit works
  • No beans? Use rice, popcorn kernels, or sunflower seeds
  • Rainy day version: Skip the window and place the seed bag on a plate near a lamp
  • Extra challenge (Logan): Draw each seed type in a "seed journal" page

🌧️ Rain Puddle Hop

15–20 min · Gross Motor + Math
›

What You'll Need

  • Blue construction paper, towels, or blue painters tape on the floor
  • Number cards (write 1–5 on index cards or paper)
  • Optional: music to play/pause for freeze rounds

How To Do It

Make 5 "puddles" on the floor using blue paper or tape. Place a number card by each one. Play music — when it stops, call a number and everyone hops to that puddle!

Variations: "Jump OVER!" "Tiptoe AROUND!" "Stomp IN!" Add animal walks between puddles — waddle like a duck, hop like a frog.

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Match numbers to puddles. "Can you jump to puddle THREE?" Count jumps: "Jump in puddle 4 exactly 4 times!" Let him call out numbers for you — he loves being in charge.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Forget numbers — just stomp and toddle between puddles. Celebrate every step. Model "jump!" (even if it's a bouncy knee bend). Chase him for giggles.

🔄 Alternatives
  • No blue paper? Use towels, washcloths, or draw circles with chalk outside
  • Outdoor version: Use chalk puddles on the driveway — add a hose for real splashing
  • Calmer version: "Puddle stepping stones" — walk carefully between, no jumping
  • Shape variation: Make puddles in different shapes — circle, square, star

🎨 Grow-a-Flower Painting

20–30 min · Art + Fine Motor
›

What You'll Need

  • Paper (any kind)
  • Washable paint or food coloring + water
  • Cotton swabs, a fork, and/or fingers
  • Green pipe cleaner or crayon for stem (optional)

How To Do It

Talk about what flowers need: sun, rain, soil. "Today we're going to GROW a flower with paint!"

  • Fork stamping — dip in paint, press for cool petal textures
  • Cotton swab dots — flower centers and raindrops
  • Finger painting — squishy, sensory, perfect for little hands

Narrate: "First the stem grows UP… then the petals open… and the sun shines down!"

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Build the flower in stages: stem → leaves → petals. Let him pick colors. "How many petals?" Try mixing: "What happens when yellow meets blue?"
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Pure sensory time — no need to make a flower. Tape paper to table or use a rimmed baking sheet. Name colors as he smooshes. Fork stamping hand-over-hand.
Fork Stamp Flowers — Easy Toddler Art
Great technique demo for Rachel
🔄 Alternatives
  • No paint? Use crayons, markers, or torn tissue paper glued on
  • Mess-free version: Put paint blobs in a ziplock bag — squish and blend without touching
  • Nature version: Collect actual petals and leaves, glue to paper as a collage
  • Extension: Press finished paintings in a heavy book overnight for a "dried flower" effect

📖 Planting a Rainbow Read-Aloud

10–15 min · Language + Faith
›

What You'll Need

  • Planting a Rainbow by Lois Ehlert (or any seed/garden book)
  • Cozy reading spot — blanket fort, couch pile, or outside

How To Do It

Read together slowly. Let them turn pages and point. Pause to ask questions: "What color is that flower? Can you find the seed?" Connect to the morning's seed activity: "Remember when WE planted seeds today?"

End with the scripture seed: "God makes things grow — just like our seeds, just like these flowers, just like YOU."

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Ask him to name the colors of each flower. "What would YOU plant in your garden?" Let him retell the story in his own words after.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) Lap time. Let him turn the pages (Ehlert's bold colors are perfect for toddlers). Point and name: "Red! Flower! Grow!" Keep it short if attention wanders — 5 minutes is great.

🔄 Alternatives
  • Don't have this book? The Tiny Seed by Eric Carle, From Seed to Plant by Gail Gibbons, or any garden book from the library
  • No book at all? Use the YouTube read-aloud above — still counts!
  • Extension: After reading, go outside and find real growing things to compare

🎵 "God Made Everything" Dance Party

10 min · Music + Movement + Faith
›

What You'll Need

  • Phone or speaker for music
  • Open floor space
  • Scarves, ribbons, or dish towels to wave (optional)

How To Do It

End the day with JOY. Put on worship music and dance together. Wave scarves like flowers blowing in the wind. Spin like seeds falling from a tree. Stomp like rain on the ground.

Between songs, freeze and name one thing God made today: "Trees!" "Caleb!" "Seeds!" "Flowers!" "Logan!" — celebrate that God made THEM too.

🦖 Logan (3y3m) Give him a scarf to wave. Let him pick the next freeze-and-name. "What did God make?" He'll surprise you with his answers.
🐻 Caleb (1y6m) He'll bounce, toddle, and wave anything you hand him. Pure joy. Hold his hands and dance together. Spin him gently. Name his movements: "Caleb is DANCING!"
